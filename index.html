Flixel Community is completely open-source, uses the MIT license and is managed through Github.

A community version. Not a replacement.

Flixel Community was born as a healthy fork of Flixel. It is not supposed to replace Flixel or to compete with it, but to co-exist. Adam (Flixel's author) encouraged us to grow a community version and maintain it to our heart's content, even offering it as an alternative to Flixel.

Our vision. Evolution.

Our mission is to maintain Flixel Community, keeping it stable and bug free. It should be as compatible as possible with Flixel. However we envision a Flixel Community using modern technologies and with new features, so we might break compatibility with Flixel. That's a price we are willing to pay for evolution.

A bunch of features. New and old ones.

Flixel Community includes a rich set of features: display thousands of moving objects, basic collisions between objects, group objects together for simplicity, easily generate and emit particles, create game levels using tilemaps, text display, save games, scrolling, mouse and keyboard input, math and color utilities.
